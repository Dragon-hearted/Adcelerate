# Shot Types & Camera Reference

## Shot Sizes

| Shot | Abbreviation | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Extreme Wide Shot | EWS | Subject tiny in vast environment | Establishing location, scale, epic feel |
| Wide Shot | WS | Full subject with surrounding environment | Context, setting the scene |
| Medium Wide | MWS | Subject from knees up | Walking, movement, lifestyle |
| Medium Shot | MS | Subject from waist up | Conversation, product interaction |
| Medium Close-Up | MCU | Subject from chest up | Emotion, talking head, testimonials |
| Close-Up | CU | Face or product fills frame | Emotion, detail, product features |
| Extreme Close-Up | ECU | Single feature/detail fills frame | Texture, micro detail, dramatic emphasis |
| Insert Shot | INS | Object or detail relevant to story | Product detail, UI screen, hands |
| Over-the-Shoulder | OTS | Camera behind one subject looking at another | Conversations, screen demos |
| Point of View | POV | Camera IS the subject's eyes | Immersive, unboxing, tutorials |

## Camera Angles

| Angle | Effect | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Eye Level | Neutral, relatable | Most scenes, conversations, testimonials |
| Low Angle | Power, dominance, heroic | Product reveal, brand authority, hero shots |
| High Angle | Vulnerability, overview | Flat lay, workspace, food, unboxing |
| Bird's Eye / Overhead | Graphic, pattern, god-view | Product layout, workspace, cooking |
| Dutch Angle (tilted) | Tension, unease, energy | Dynamic ads, music videos, action |
| Worm's Eye | Extreme power, towering | Architecture, dramatic product shots |

## Camera Movement (for NanoBanana Prompt Phrasing)

NanoBanana generates still images, so describe the FEELING of movement rather than literal motion:

| Movement | Conveys | Prompt Phrasing |
|---|---|---|
| Static | Stability, focus, confidence | "Locked-off composition", "Steady, grounded frame" |
| Pan | Reveal, exploration, scope | "Sweeping horizontal view", "Panoramic perspective" |
| Tilt | Scale, grandeur, discovery | "Looking upward", "Towering vertical perspective" |
| Dolly In | Intimacy, focus, tension | "Drawing closer to subject", "Intimate proximity" |
| Pull Back | Context reveal, surprise | "Revealing wider environment", "Pulling back to show scale" |
| Tracking | Energy, movement, pursuit | "Dynamic angle alongside subject", "Movement energy" |
| Crane / Drone | Scale, production value | "Aerial perspective", "Elevated sweeping viewpoint" |
| Handheld | Authenticity, urgency, rawness | "Organic handheld feel", "Intimate documentary style" |

## Composition Principles

Use these when writing visual direction and NanoBanana prompts:

| Principle | Description | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Rule of Thirds | Subject at intersection of 3x3 grid | Default for most scenes |
| Center Frame | Subject dead center | Power, symmetry, product hero shots |
| Leading Lines | Lines guide eye to focal point | Environmental scenes, depth |
| Negative Space | Breathing room around subject | Elegance, minimalism, text overlay space |
| Symmetry | Mirror balance | Power, formality, architecture |
| Frame within Frame | Environment frames the subject | Depth, intimacy, voyeuristic feel |
| Depth Layers | Foreground / midground / background | Richness, cinematic depth |
| Diagonal | Subject or lines on diagonal | Energy, dynamism, tension |
| Fill the Frame | Subject fills entire frame | Impact, detail, intimacy |

## NanoBanana-Specific Notes

- Always specify depth of field: "shallow depth of field" (subject isolation) or "deep focus" (everything sharp)
- Name specific colors rather than vague descriptions: "warm amber tones" not "nice colors"
- Describe the mood through lighting, not adjectives: "soft golden light through gauze curtains" not "romantic feeling"
- End every prompt with "No text in image" to avoid garbled text artifacts
- For product shots: name the product specifically and describe its physical appearance in detail
