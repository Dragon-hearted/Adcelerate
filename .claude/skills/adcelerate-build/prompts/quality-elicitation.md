# Quality Elicitation Prompts

Use these prompts during Step 2 (Knowledge Capture), Phase 2, to understand quality standards. The goal is to extract what "good" looks like from the engineer's perspective.

## Quality Definition

- "When you look at the final output, what makes you say 'this is good'?"
- "What would make you reject output and redo it?"
- "What's the difference between 'acceptable' and 'excellent'?"
- "If you had to grade output on a scale, what separates an A from a B? A B from a C?"

## Standards & Expectations

- "Are there industry standards or client expectations to meet?"
- "Do different clients have different quality bars?"
- "Has the quality bar changed over time? What drove those changes?"
- "What's the minimum quality you'd ship to a client?"

## Past Issues

- "What quality issues have you seen in the past?"
- "What are the most common quality complaints?"
- "Has output ever gone to a client that you weren't happy with? What was wrong?"
- "What mistakes are easy to make but hard to catch?"

## Training Perspective

- "If you were training someone to do this, what would you emphasize?"
- "What would you tell a new person to watch out for?"
- "What takes the longest to learn? What's the hardest part to get right?"
- "What do experienced people do differently from beginners?"

## Quality Inspection

- "How do you check quality today? What's your review process?"
- "What do you look at first when reviewing output?"
- "Are there quick tells that something is wrong?"
- "How long does quality review take? What makes it faster or slower?"

## Trade-offs

- "Where do you trade off quality for speed?"
- "What quality compromises are acceptable? Which are not?"
- "If you had unlimited time, what would you do differently?"
- "What's 'good enough' vs. what's 'worth the extra effort'?"
